Merchant Restrictions.

Merchant restrictions is a setting within the meal credit program that allows admins to prevent company-funded credits from being used at non-restaurant merchants, such as grocery or liquor stores, giving clients greater control over how their meal perks are used by corporate diners.

    User & Business Needs

    User: Control over where employees spend their meal credits
    Business: Become the most flexible meal perks program to retain and acquire clients

    Impact

    Retained and protected $50+ million in annual spend, and unlocked an annual estimate of $7 million in Gross Food Sales by our enterprise product tier

    Contribution

    Lead designer in collaboration with PM, UX writer and engineers

Problem

Grubhub Corporate positions itself as “the industry’s most flexible meal perks platform,” and clients expect granular control over how company-funded meal credits are spent, especially when it comes to restricting purchases from grocery, convenience, and other non-restaurant merchants. However, this level of control didn’t exist; clients could only rely on internal policy reminders, hoping employees would follow them. To address this gap, I introduced merchant restrictions, allowing admins to customize meal credit settings and exclude specific merchant categories.

Challenge: Client admins are frustrated by the lack of in-product controls to enforce ordering policies, making it difficult to prevent employees from using company funds at certain merchant types.

Process

I collaborated with PM and engineering through workshops to align on business goals, user needs, and technical constraints. We scoped the initiative into milestones—first delivering merchant-level restrictions as a near-term solution, with plans to introduce more granular item-level controls in future phases.

Research

I started off by working with our client success team to talk to client admins and get their thoughts on how the meal credit program is meeting their needs. Then, I researched our competitors that offer meal credit programs to corporate clients and analyzed how they restrict meal credits being used on orders on the merchant level.

    Voice of Customers

    Talked and identified 50+ clients at risk of churn that gave direct feedback on the meal credit program

    Competitor Analysis

    Analyzed our competitor's meal credit program and how they set restrictions on meal credits

Synthesize

As I moved into synthesis, I mapped out key touchpoints across the Grubhub experience where both admins and diners might encounter merchant restrictions. This helped surface opportunities to ensure the feature is intuitive and clearly communicated. From this, I identified core design takeaways to guide the experience.

Design Takeaways

    Flexible Options

    Admins need to be able to restrict different types of merchant from different levels in their corporate account

    Clear Definitions

    Merchant-level restriction is relatively broad, so clarity on the definition is essential for both admins and diners

    Undisruptive Experience

    Even with restrictions in place, diners should still have the flexibility to place orders from their preferred merchants

Design Challenge

How might we empower admins to restrict meal credits by item and merchant while ensuring clear, transparent communication of these restrictions to diners?

Solution

On the admin side, I added a dedicated section within the account and meal credit settings, giving admins control over which merchant types are eligible for meal credits. On the diner side, I designed clear labels and contextual error messages in the Grubhub app to help diners understand why certain merchants may be unavailable. These updates ensure both parties stay informed and aligned.

Restricting Merchants in Account Settings

Admins can now restrict meal credits from being used at convenience stores, grocery stores, pet stores, florists, pharmacies, liquor stores, and office supply stores. These restrictions are set at the account level and automatically apply to all meal credits associated with the account.

Setting up merchant restrictions on the account-level.

Restricting Merchants in Meal Credit Settings

Admins who want to set different merchant restrictions for specific meal credits can configure them directly within the meal credit settings. These settings offer the same merchant type options available at the account level, allowing for more tailored control.

Setting merchant restrictions in the meal credit-level.

Communicating Restrictions on the Diner Side

Once merchant restrictions are applied to a meal credit, corporate diners will see the details in their Grubhub app wallet. At checkout, if their cart contains items from restricted merchants, they can still place the order, but the corporate meal credit will not be applied. An error message will clearly communicate the applicable restrictions.

Clear merchant restriction details and error messaging in the diner experience.

Merchant Restrictions on the Mobile Experience

Since the majority of corporate diners place their orders on the mobile app, the merchant restriction experience mirrors that of the desktop version and appears across the same touchpoints.
