Digital Commercial Bank

Digital Commercial Bank is the new digital banking platform for the Commercial Banking segment, specifically for startups. The platform not only accommodates their growing challenges as they scale, but also provides a new banking experience that helps clients get aquinted with the platform.

    User & Business Needs

    User: Find a digital banking platform that's easy to use for startups
    Business: Deliver an MVP in a tight timeframe to onboard startup clients

    Impact

    Shipped an MVP, received over $250 million in funding to accelerate the full launch of Digital Commercial Bank, got 50+ clients onboarded on the beta version

    Contribution

    Drove product strategy. Lead designer for dashboard, payments, reports and account visibility in collaboration with PM and engineers

Problem

The Commercial Banking (CB) segment at JPMC had traditionally relied on legacy platforms like Connect and Access to serve its clients. However, as startups became one of CB's fastest-growing client segments, it became clear that these platforms weren’t meeting their needs. Many startup users struggled with complex financial tools and unfamiliar banking terminology. So in response, we began developing a new digital banking platform specifically tailored to startups.

Challenge: Startup clients are unable to bank with confidence using JPMC’s legacy banking platforms, because of their limited knowledge in financial tools and banking terminologies.

Process

Our design team collaboratively defined the product strategy and identified the core functionalities to prioritize for the MVP. I led the design efforts for key areas including the dashboard, payments, reporting, documents, and account visibility.

Research

I started off by talking to startup clients and bankers who help with these clients to learn about their experience using JPMC legacy banking platforms. Then, I conducted heuristic analysis to evaluate the usability of Chase Connect to identify opportunities for improvement. And lastly, I researched competitors within the digital banking platform space to understand what makes them popular among the startup community.

Synthesize

Once I concluded my research, I highlighted the pain points of using Chase Connect and identified opportunities for improvement to define design requirements for the new banking platform. Here are the design requirements and the HMW statement:

Design Requirements

    Low Barrier to Entry

    Allow new clients to easily find the features they need to complete their banking tasks and not feel overwhelmed by them

    User-friendly Banking Language

    Use user-friendly languages to help bring familiarity while increasing confidence in themselves and the bank

    Customize and Configure

    Customize the platform to prioritize what they want to see and configure their financial data

Design Challenge

How might we provide a new banking experience that allows startup clients to quickly get acquainted with the platform and its core features to complete their banking tasks based on their business needs?

Solution

Using a new design system, we created a digital banking platform with core functionalities designed for startup clients. We translated banking terminologies to user language to help their understanding, and redesigned the information architecture to be clear, comprehensive and simple.

Dashboard

A snapshot of your account health with core metrics, which is also customizable where all information pertinent to the user's role is visible.
