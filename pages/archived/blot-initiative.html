BLOT Initiative.

BLOT, Building Life Over Time, is a green space initiative determined to bring more life into urban settings and improve air quality and the overall community. We created a brand identity and designed promotional materials and wireframes to establish the initiative with the objective to raise awareness of our work.

Building Life Over Time.

Based on a 2017 study conducted by "Crosstown Health" which examined 250 neigborhoods in LA county. Chinatown had the worse air quality, due to its location being surrounded by three major highways: CA-110, the I-10, and the I-5. Chinatown is one of the most popular places in LA and many people go there to enjoy Chinese cuisine. However, the pollution is deterioating many people's health, making the trip to Chinatown risky. We want to change that with our initiative.

BLOT believes that if people can live in cities, so can nature.

The challenge of this project is to address a social issue within our society using design strategy supported by research from case studies. Our team narrowed down our choices to target urban settings and address the need of improving living conditions by integrating sustainable solutions to the targeted area. We think green space is the best option for the urban setting solution.

To determine how our initiative introduce green space to Chinatown, our team researched on existing green space initiatives to understand how they do it. There are countless of green space initiatives around the world and these three are the ones that we took a closer look at.

Gardens By the Bay

Singapore

Gardens By the Bay is a nature park that spans 101 hectares (250 acres) of reclaimed land. It is part of Singapore's plan to transform its "Garden City" to a "City of a Garden". It aims to raise the quality of life by enhancing greenery and flora in the city.

The High Line

New York City

The High Line is a 1.45-mile-long elevated linear park, greenway and rail trail built on a former New York Central Railroad. The railroad was repurposed into a public park that is integrated in the urban settings of west side of Manhattan.

Edifício Santalaia

Bogotá

Edifício Santalaia is a high end multi-family residental building. The establisment was high praised for one the most beautiful and largest Vertical Gardens in the world. The lush green wall is a reminder that cities should not always only be covered in concrete, but should constantly relate with nature instead.

BLOT paid a visit to Chinatown to see how green space best fit in this urban setting.

Our team conducted an on-site research to see which areas in Chinatown is suitable to build a green space. The two locations to implement our green design were a parking structure and an empty field in the Central Plaza.

Make BLOT and its work known throughout the environmental committee.

In order to distinguish ourselves from other environmental organisations, BLOT needed an identity. BLOT stands for "Building Life Over Time", so we thought using a leaf would best represent our idea of creating life in urban settings. So we took a look at organic forms using leaf motif.

We came up with a style guide for BLOT which consists of logo designs, color palettes and typography. Since our initiative does extensive research on finding plants that are suitable to grow in a city, we want our identity to emphasis our research and speciality.

Now, BLOT has an identity, but no one is aware of its existence.

BLOT needs to be promoted, since our work highly depends on people's donation and contribution to our cause. So we decided to host a grand opening event to get BLOT started. The opening event will be promoted through event invitational cards and social media. We want the cards to be as eco-friendly as possible, so we created graphics to be printed on wildflower seed paper.

Follow BLOT on Instagram, like their work and spread the word!

There is no faster way to promote anything than through social media. It provides information to anyone anywhere at anytime, so we want make BLOT's presence noticable by promoting the opening event and its identity.

Instagram is one of most used social media, so we thought this was a good place to start. We created digital assets to launch social media campaigns.

Want to be a part of the initiative? We designed a web platform, so environmentalists like you can see what is BLOT up to.

First stop, Chinatown. Next stop...

BLOT initiative was created to address the air pollution in Chinatown, but that doesn't mean our work stops there. The initiative is not limited to Chinatown nor Los Angeles, so in the future, we may undertake projects in different cities, such as New York or Nashville. The need for green space is becoming more significant everyday and we want to find sustainable ways to improve air quality without having to disrupting people's urban lifestyle.
