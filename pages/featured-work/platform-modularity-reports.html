Platform Modularity Reports.

Reporting in Chase Connect allows clients to create balance and transactions reports for their bank accounts. With Platform Modularity, clients with J.P. Morgan Access accounts are now able to create Access reports within Connect.

Problem

Emerging Middle Market (EMM) clients enjoy the simplicity of Chase Connect. However, when clients move to J.P. Morgan Access or set up both for richer product features, it results in dissatisfaction due to Access being difficult to use. With Platform Modularity, it addresses the issue by unlocking specific Access features within the Connect experience, and my job is to design that experience for creating Access reports in Connect.

Challenge: How might we help EMM clients create Access reports, which are more robust and complicated, within the Connect experience?

Process

Research

Connect has its own report creation and my job is to make it possible to create Access reports within the Connect experience. I started off by analyzing the report creation process in both Connect and Access to understand the experience. Then, I looked at past research traces, initial user flows and designs that were explored.

    Analyzed reports creation process in both Connect and Access

    Prior Work

    Reviewed past research, user flows and wireframes

Synthesize

After going through the prior work and evaluating the two banking platforms, I began to formulate takeaways to guide my design and identify the challenge that addressing. Here are the design takeaways and HMW statement:

Design Takeaways

    Simplify the Complexity

    Simplify reports, so users would not need to rely on detailed instructions to use the module

    Clear User Flows

    Allow users to know what is available to them and what action to take next

    Reuse Reports

    Allow users to easily locate and reuse their favorite reports

Design Challenge

How might we streamline the report creation process that allows EMM clients to easily navigate through the available actions and reuse their favorite reports after they completed the process?

Ideate & Design

Before I went into design, I mapped out different user flows with the PMs to ensure that we cover all the product requirements, while also exploring for the most streamlined reporting flow.

User Flows

I proposed an integrated experience that allowed users to switch between Connect and Access reports during the creation process. Here's a high-level flow of how this experience works:

  • User chooses to create between U.S. (Connect) and International (Access) reports, and they'll have to fill out the respective fields
  • Once a report is created, it will generate a report file. Users can edit or run these reports again
  • All the generated report files will be organized in a table for users to perform actions, like download and delete

Full user flow of integrated reporting.

Create New Report

There are 3 steps in creating a report: Basic Information, Data & Display and Schedule. The 3-step process helps guide clients to fill out information like choosing between a US or international report, what transaction type they want to see and if they want the report be placed on a schedule.

3-step process to create a report.

Saved Reports

Once a report is created, it will automatically generate a report file, unless scheduled for another time. Due to limited capabilities and feedback from prior research, only US reports will be saved in the left navigation under a collapsable tab. Clients can edit or rerun these reports by assigning them to a new schedule.

US reports are saved and displayed on the left navigation.

View Report Files

Report files are stored and organized in a table which can be switched between US and international using the dropdown menu. Clients can download these report files in PDF or CSV to share with others, or delete them if they no longer need the files.

Generated report files are stored and organized in a switchable table.

Usability Testing

I talked to 3 clients in finance positions and they loved the simplicity of creating an international report on Connect. However, there were some confusion around the scheduling options and they would also like to be able to save international reports. Here were the 3 major improvements made:

  • Removed unpopular schedule frequency options, e.g. quarterly, on event and on specific dates
  • Created a new page to store reports in a table that can be switched between US and international reports, similar to "View/Download Reports" (renamed from "View My Reports")
  • Updated the experience for editing a report

Conclusion

After handing off the designs to the tech team, platform modularity reports became available for EMM clients to use. This was an interesting project to work on because reporting is relatively straighforward but it was challenging to combine Access and Connect report creations due to how differently they work. My takeaway would be to fully understand the product before going into the design, which is essential moving forward with designing reports on a new banking platform.
