Merchant Restrictions.

Merchant restrictions is a setting within the meal benefit program that enables admins to prevent meal credits from being used at non-restaurant merchants, like grocery and liquor stores, which will give clients more control over the type of purchase made by their account users with company funds.

    User & Business Needs

    User: Control over the type of purchase made by their account users
    Business: Become industry's most flexible meal perks platform

    Impact

    Retained and protected $50+ million in annual spend, and unlocked an annual estimate of $7 million in Gross Food Sales by our enterprise product tier

    Contribution

    Lead designer in collaboration with PM, UX writer and engineers

Problem

Grubhub Corporate's current market claim to fame is that we are "the industry's most flexible meal perks platform". As such, clients expect fine grain control of the type of purchases made by their account users with company funds, especially purchases from grocery, convenience, & other retail merchants. However, in reality, such a feature does not exist and clients can only communicate company's order policies to their employees in the hopes that they would adhere to them. So, we're introducing the ability for clients to customize meal credit settings to exclude certain merchant types.

Challenge: How might we enhance the meal credit setting to enforce meal credits from being used on certain merchant types?

Process

We broke down the product into multiple milestones based on use case priority, and we've launched the first milestone to restrict meal credits from being used on order on the merchant level. This is still an ongoing initiative to expand on other capabilities.

Research

I started off by working with our client success team to talk to client admins and get their thoughts on how the meal credit program is meeting their needs. Then, I researched our competitors that offer meal credit programs to corporate clients and analyzed how they restrict meal credits being used on orders on the merchant level.

    Voice of Customers

    Talked and identified 50+ clients at risk of churn that gave direct feedback on the meal credit program

    Competitor Analysis

    Analyzed our competitor's meal credit program and how they restrict merchants

Synthesize

Going into synthesis, I identified touch points within Grubhub where admins and diners may encounter merchant restrictions, and also key design takeaways that will make merchant restrictions easy to use and understand. Here are the design takeaways and HMW statement:

Design Takeaways

    Flexible Options

    Admins need to be able to restrict different types of merchant from different levels in their corporate account

    Clear Definitions

    Merchant-level restriction is relatively general, so clarity on the definition of merchant types is essential

    Restriction Confirmation

    Diners will need to be able to know which merchant types they are not able to order from using their meal credits

Design Challenge

How might we introduce new administrative controls to restrict meal credits from being used at certain merchants in a way that clearly communicates these restrictions to both admins and diners?

Solution

Within the corporate account, I added a new section in the account and meal credit settings, allowing admins to restrict meal credits from being used at certain merchant types. On the diner side, I introduced new labels and error messages in the Grubhub app to clearly communicate these restrictions, ensuring diners stay informed about their meal credit usage. Here are the touchpoints with the new design:

Restrictions in Account Settings

Admins can now restrict meal credits from being used at certain merchants, including convenience stores, grocery stores, pet stores, florists, pharmacies, liquor stores, and office supply stores. These restrictions, set at the account level, will apply to all meal credits associated with the account.

Restricting merchants in corporate account settings.

Restrictions in Meal Credit Settings

Admins who want to set different merchant restrictions for specific meal credits can do so within the meal credit settings, which offer the same merchant type options as the account settings.

Restricting merchants in meal credit settings.

Restrictions in Grubhub's Wallet

Once merchant restrictions are applied to a meal credit, corporate diners will be notified in their Grubhub app wallet. Using a copy formula, the restriction message dynamically updates based on the selected merchant types.

Merchant restrictions message and copy formula in Wallet.

Restrictions in Grubhub's Checkout

If diners have items from restricted merchants in their cart at checkout, they can still place the order, but their corporate meal credits will not be applied. An error message will appear in the "Corporate line of credits" section to inform them of the merchant restrictions for this credit.

Error messages to inform diners of the meal credit's merchant restrictions.
