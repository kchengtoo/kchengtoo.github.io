Platform Modularity Reports.

Reporting in Chase Connect allows clients to create balance and transactions reports for their bank accounts. With Platform Modularity, clients with J.P. Morgan Access accounts are now able to create Access reports within Connect.

    User & Business Needs

    User: A simplier way to create Access reports.
    Business: Improve reports conversion rate

    Impact

    Drop rate to create Access reports decreased by 300% and well-received by users with a high NPS

    Contribution

    Lead designer and led the collaboration efforts with PMs and engineers

Problem

Emerging Middle Market (EMM) clients enjoy the simplicity of Chase Connect. However, when they move to J.P. Morgan Access for richer product features, it results in dissatisfaction due to Access being difficult to use. With Platform Modularity, it addresses the issue by unlocking specific Access features within the Connect experience, and my job is to design that experience for creating Access reports in Connect.

Challenge: How might we help EMM clients create Access reports, which are more robust yet complicated, within the simplicity of the Connect experience?

Research

Connect has its own report creation and my job is to design an experience that integrates Access reporting with Connect's. I started off by analyzing the reporting process in both platforms to get an understanding of their capabilities. Then, I looked at past research traces, initial user flows and designs that were explored to see what can be built upon.

    Platform Audit

    Analyzed reports creation process in both Connect and Access

    Prior Work

    Reviewed past research, user flows and designs

Synthesize

After my research findings, I formulated takeaways about how clients create their reports using what's available in Access and Connects, and identified the challenge to guide my design. Here are the design takeaways and HMW statement:

Design Takeaways

    Simplify the Complexity

    Simplify reports, so users would not need to rely on detailed instructions to use the module

    Clear User Flows

    Allow users to know what is available to them and what action to take next

    Reuse Reports

    Allow users to easily locate and reuse their favorite reports

Design Challenge

How might we streamline the report creation process that allows EMM clients to easily navigate through the available actions and reuse their favorite reports after they completed the process?

Ideate & Design

Before I went into design, I mapped out different user flows with the PMs to ensure that we cover all the product requirements, while also exploring for the most streamlined reporting flow.

Information Architecture

I proposed an integrated experience that allowed users to switch between Connect and Access reports during the creation process. Here's a high-level structure of how this experience works:

  • User chooses to create between U.S. (Connect) and International (Access) reports, and they'll have to fill out the respective fields
  • Once a report is created, it will generate a report file. Users can edit or run these reports again
  • All the generated report files will be organized in a table for users to perform actions, like download and delete

Information architecture of integrated reporting.

Create New Report

There are 3 steps in creating a report: Basic Information, Data & Display and Schedule. The 3-step process helps guide clients to fill out information like choosing between a US or international report, what transaction type they want to see and if they want to place the report on a schedule.

3-step process to create a report.

Saved Reports

Once a report is created, it will automatically generate a report file, unless scheduled for another time. Due to limited capabilities, only US reports can be saved in the left navigation under a collapsible tab. Clients can edit or rerun these reports by assigning them to a new schedule.

US reports are saved and displayed on the left navigation.

View Report Files

Report files are stored and organized in a table which can be switched between US and international using the dropdown menu. Clients can download these report files in PDF or CSV to share with others, or delete them if they no longer need the files.

Generated report files are stored and organized in a switchable table.

Usability Testing

I talked to 3 clients and they loved the simplicity of creating an international report on Connect. However, there was some confusion around the scheduling options and they would also like to be able to save international reports. Here were the 3 major improvements made:

  • Removed unpopular schedule frequency options, e.g. quarterly, on event and on specific dates
  • Created a new page to store reports in a table that can be switched between US and international, similar to the report files table (renamed to "View/Download Reports")
  • Updated the experience for editing a report

Impact

We designed the success metrics from the beginning, and measured the success from both the user and business side.

  • Drop rate decrease: Majority of the clients stopped at the scheduling step when creating reports on Access. After the new reports was launched, drop-out rate dropped from 60% to 20%
  • Time-on-task: Based on A/B testing results, the report creation time decreased from 6:30min to 4:15min
  • User satisfaction: Our bankers and clients appreciate the simplicity of the new Access reports creation, with an NPS of +30
