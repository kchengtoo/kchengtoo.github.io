Arity.

Arity is a mobility data and analytics company that provides highly predictive insights and they were interested in embedding their SDK into existing fitness apps. I worked on prototypes of three concept features that provide unique workout experience on Strava, a social fitness app that tracks human exercise, by incorporating Arity’s data platform.

Problem

Arity was interested in the fitness industry due to the large number of apps and active users in that space, which they can gather more robust insights about the way people move. Therefore, we were charged with understanding how users interact with fitness apps, so Arity can prioritize which capabilities to build and add value to the experience that users look for from these apps.

Challenge: How might Arity create a unique fitness experience for users that encourages them to share data and affords the company a valuable partnership?

Research

We conducted current state app assessment to understand the landscape of possibilities for creating digital experiences that work for Arity. We also conducted user interviews with people who use fitness apps in their workouts, and we learned their fitness behaviors, usage patterns, and mindsets and motivation for sharing data.

    Current App Analysis

    Analyzed 7 fitness apps using 3 frameworks

    User Interview

    Conducted 45 minutes sessions with 8 participants

Synthesize

After talking to participants about their fitness routines and their experiences with fitness apps, we began to identify what users want in their fitness routine. Here were the insights and our proposal to Arity for app partnership:

Insights

    Safe Use of Data

    Users see the value of sharing data in fitness apps to track their activity, but they need to feel safe while using these features

    Community Engagement

    Users feel connected and motivated to maintain health when they engage with their friends online through the fitness app

    Variability

    Users seek guidance from apps because they want variation in their fitness routine to become more well-rounded and refreshing

App Partnership

Based on our app analysis and interviews with fitness app users, we decided to go with Strava, because it has a large user base, and safety and mobility-related features that compliment well with Arity's capabilities.

Strava, a social fitness app that tracks human exercises.

Design

Going forward with Strava, we began to design features, keyed into our insights of safe data usage, community engagement and variability, that will provide unique experience on the app that will benefit its users. Here were the concepts that we decided to go with:

  • Conditions - Filter through route conditions based on air quality, traffic, terrain, and weather to view updated Suggested Routes
  • Beacon Plus - Detect an accident during a workout and alert emergency contacts

Conditions

This feature works as a new filter on the Explore Routes page. Users can use this feature by clicking on the Conditions tab and select preferences that will filter and update the route choices.

User flow and lo-fi wireframes of Conditions.

Beacon Plus

Strava has a feature called Beacon that allows users to set their emergency contacts. With Beacon Plus, their phone will monitior the user's workout and send notifications to their contacts if the user gets into an accident.

There are two parts for Beacon Plus and the first part is enabling the feature. Users can do this by toggling on Beacon Plus and add emergency contacts that the app will contact if the user gets into an accident during their workout.

User flow and lo-fi wireframes of Beacon Plus.

Accident Detection

Accident Detection is the second part of the Beacon Plus feature. When user's phone detects an accident during their workout, Strava will display a button to immediately notify their safety contact and a button to dismiss the detection. If the user does not click on either button within a minute, the app will automatically notify their contacts.

User flow and lo-fi wireframes of Accident Detection.

Usability Testing

We gathered 10 participants to go through our user testing and realized that some parts of the features lack clarity and spontaneousness. Users were expecting more explicit indicators in which they would not have to look twice, while at the same time wanting a more "conceptual" experience when using the features.

  • Redesigned the condition preferences with more conversational style
  • Enabled selection for different contact methods and displayed selected methods on the Accident Detection cell
  • Enlarged the countdown timer and integrated sound notification during the Accident Detection screen

Conclusion

We received great feedback on our design from Arity and how we incorporated our client and user's needs into the design. If we had more time, I would add another round of user testing with the final concept features. We tested low-fidelity of the features with our users, but after we made new iterations based on their initial feedback, we didn’t get more from them. So, it would be more insightful to add a final round of user testing before presenting the prototypes to our client.
