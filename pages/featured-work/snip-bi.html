Snip BI.

Snip BI is a business intelligence (BI) tool that gives managers real-time visibility into how teams spend their time. By mapping insights to the org chart and automating reporting across internal tools, it surfaces actionable productivity trends and enables faster, data-driven decisions at every level.

    User & Business Needs

    User: A quick, accurate view of team productivity without manual effort
    Business: Real-time, data-driven insights to optimize performance and decisions

    Impact

    Reduced manual reporting time by 40%, saving managers 10 hours per week, and enhanced productivity visibility to improve resource allocation efficiency by 15%.

    Contribution

    Lead designer in collaboration with founder and engineers

Problem

As modern teams work across dozens of tools, enterprise leaders lack clear, real-time visibility into how work gets done. Traditional BI solutions offer fragmented data, while existing tools rely on manual input, slowing decisions and obscuring productivity trends. Mathpix saw an opportunity to help clients close this gap by designing an AI-powered reporting tool built around the org chart. By combining their document processing AI, tool integrations, screen tracking, and AI agents, this solution would automate reporting and deliver actionable insights tailored to managers.

Challenge: Enterprise leaders lack real-time, org-aware visibility into how teams work across tools. The challenge is to design an AI-powered BI tool that automates reporting and delivers actionable productivity insights without adding manual overhead.

Process

I researched user needs and business challenges related to productivity reporting, defined the core problem, and designed AI-powered, org-chart-based reporting concepts. After iterating based on user feedback, I collaborated with cross-functional teams to prepare designs for implementation.

Research

I conducted user interviews and workflow analysis to understand how managers track productivity. I identified key pain points in manual reporting and gaps in existing BI tools, then mapped user needs across roles. Competitive research helped uncover whitespace for automation and AI-powered insights.

Synthesize

I synthesized research findings to identify core user needs: uncovering blockers, accessing concise insights, and relying on metric-driven content. These insights informed the product direction and prioritized features that automate data collection and deliver actionable, decision-ready reporting for managers.

Design Considerations

    Uncovering Blockers

    Help managers quickly identify workflow bottlenecks or stalled projects

    Concise Insights

    Deliver high-signal summaries tailored to what each manager needs to know

    Metric-Driven Content

    Present key performance data visually to support fast, informed decisions

Design Challenge

How might we design an AI-powered reporting experience that helps users quickly surface actionable employee insights and clearly understand their implications?

Solution

I designed a business intelligence tool that maps productivity insights to a company’s org chart and automates reporting across integrated tools. The solution uses AI to surface concise, metric-driven insights and uncover potential blockers, which gives managers real-time visibility without adding reporting overhead.

AI-Powered Reporting Tool

Managers can quickly understand how their teams are working through a centralized dashboard that surfaces high-level insights. A simple, intuitive information architecture makes it easy to navigate across teams, view employee lists, and drill down into individual reports.

Style Guide + Design System

I developed a lightweight design system to ensure consistency, clarity, and scalability across the platform. The system included a clean, data-focused visual style, reusable UI components, and guidelines for layout, typography, and color usage. It was built to support quick iteration while maintaining visual cohesion across dashboards, reports, and employee views.
