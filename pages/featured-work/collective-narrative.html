Collective Narrative.

Collective Narrative (CN) is a novel social media app that identifies users in a situation and automatically notifies them to participate in a collective storytelling experience. By contributing to the narrative and reading other contributions, it helps users feel a sense of togetherness with their community.

Jun '21 Paper: The first paper on Collective Narrative: Scaffolding Community Storytelling through Context Awareness is accepted at CSCW'21.

Problem

Going through shared experiences together, like pulling an all-nighter in the library with fellow students before a paper deadline, is an incredible way for communities to bond. However, this sense of togetherness becomes lost when communities go virtual, even though they're still pulling the same all-nighter. We explored how this sense of togetherness can be recreated in collective storytelling experiences, so communities can bond at distance.

Challenge: How might we create a sense of togetherness in social storytelling experiences to help communities bond at distance?

Process

Coming into this project, some work had been done by previous students who conducted research on their use cases for CN. We picked up from there by coming up with new use cases based on needfinding study, defining design arguments and building a working prototype for further user study.

The research process took over six months to complete.

Research

We looked at the results from the latest study to frame our needfinding study, in which we conducted user interviews with Northwestern students on how they share experience and connect with their communities. During the interview, we also showed a list of themes and examples of topics to see what use case participants are interested in participating.

    Prior Work

    Analyzed results from previous studies

    Needfinding Study

    Conducted 40 minutes interview with 4 participants

Synthesize

After the research, we began to identify motivations for students to share their experiences with the community and use case that will frame our social storytelling experience. Here were the insights and use case to guide our solution:

Insights

    Identity Interests

    Students are interested in identity-based communities, as they want to learn more about communities that they identify with

    Related Experiences

    Students are willing to share their stories on related experiences when they feel strong emotions to them

    Valued by Community

    Interaction and connection between members get deepened when they feel valued by their community

Use Case

From our needfinding study, students were interested in the theme on finals week, because many students experience the same struggle and they want to hear stories of others during that period of time. So, we chose Northwestern students during finals week as our use case and targeted towards senior students.

Senior students during finals week at Northwestern University.

Design & Test I

Using context-awareness system from the branching research concept, we wanted CN to be able to organize individual experiences into a collective and engaging story.

Narrative Structure

Prior study results indicated that users were not able to feel a sense of togetherness from reading the narrative artifact, because CN did not organize diverse moments shared by users into a comprehensive story that other users within the community can relate to. With that in mind, we came up with a "side-by-side timelines" narrative structure as our solution.

"Side-by-side timelines" narrative structure.

User Study I

To verify the design of the narrative structure, we tested it with 3 participants. While the content in the prototype were samples of students' experiences during the finals week, participants found that they were too generic and diversity in experience was not well reflected in the narrative. Here were some of the changes we've made:

  • Incorporated a system that will help users share more specific experiences during their finals week
  • Changed the timeline from throughout the day to the week for more diverse experiences

Hi-fi wireframes of the narrative artifact and post page.

Design & Test II

While the first design and study mainly focused on the narrative artifact, many feedbacks aimed on how CN would collect students' experiences. So, here's what we focused on in this iteration:

  • Designed the user flow to show the interfaces that users will interact with they share their experiences on CN
  • Formulated pre-story prompts and questions that will guide users to submit contributions that are more related to the theme

User Flow

We sketched out the interfaces that will guide users to share their experiences on CN, which were Notification, Pre-Story Questions, Story Prompts and Contribution, and the narrative artifact. This sketch also helped us to plan the execution for the working prototype that we used in the second study.

User flow of Collective Narrative with tech implementation notes.

Pre-Story Setup

Pre-Story Questions and Story Prompts are the key interfaces that will help users set up their contribution. In Pre-Story Questions, we came up with a set of questions that asks about user's emotional state or atmosphere of a place, which will help CN understand their specific context.

Selecting a response in Pre-Story Questions.

Once users answer the questions, they will move to Story Prompts to submit a photo and a description of their moment. We came up with a set of prompts that will be dynamically generated determined by the identified context and their responses from the pre-story questions. These prompts will scaffold users to share experience and feelings better for their specific context.

Submitting a photo and a description of user's moment in Story Prompts.

User Study II

We recruited 14 participants to go through a 4-day long study where users downloaded the CN app on their iPhones and interacted with it as they went about their daily routines. After the study, users filled out a survey and we did 5 follow-up interviews with actively engaged users. Here's what we learned:

  • CNâ€™s context-aware prompting increased users' confidence and awareness to share moments that they normally wouldn't on other social media
  • The spontaneity and authenticity of posts on CN were key factors that helped the users feel connected with other seniors
  • Some users did not feel changes in their connection with their community, because CN did not support subsequent interactions with other users after seeing the narrative
