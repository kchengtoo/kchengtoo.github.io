Tiered Accounts.

Tiered accounts establish a parent-child hierarchy within Grubhub Corporate Accounts. This structure enables the onboarding team to use a single parent account as a template and efficiently generate multiple child accounts beneath it, ideal for large clients with complex, multi-level organizations.

    User & Business Needs

    User: Streamline the process to create a parent account with multiple children
    Business: Obtain the ability to support large Enterprise clients

    Impact

    Unlocked an annual estimate of $1.2 million in Gross Food Sales and reduced the time it takes to create a parent account with 50 child accounts by 95%

    Contribution

    Lead designer in collaboration with PM and engineers

Problem

Within our Corporate Enterprise client base, many organizations operate with multiple brands, subsidiaries, or locations, each requiring tailored meal benefits through Grubhub Corporate Accounts. However, the previous lack of a hierarchical structure in our system made it difficult for Customer Success Managers (CSMs) to effectively support these large, complex clients. To address this, we're enhancing the corporate account structure to introduce multi-tiered support, enabling us to better serve clients with layered organizational models.

Challenge: Due to the lack of hierarchical structure and advanced administrative capabilities within Grubhub’s corporate accounts, CSMs have struggled to effectively support large enterprise clients with complex organizational needs.

Research

I started by interviewing client admins responsible for managing their company’s Grubhub Corporate Accounts to understand the needs of large enterprises. In parallel, I spoke with our Customer Success Managers (CSMs) to learn about their experiences onboarding complex organizations and to assess the current limitations of creating a hierarchical structure within corporate accounts.

    Stakeholder Interview

    Talked to 9 CSMs and 5 client admins of their company's corporate account

    Heuristic Analysis

    Evaluated the current process for creating a parent account with multiple child accounts

Synthesize

After completing my research, I identified key design takeaways that revealed opportunities to enhance the corporate account experience for large enterprise clients. These insights helped shape the following design requirements and HMW statement:

Design Takeaways

    No-True Hierarchy

    CSMs have been relying on a workaround that forms a pseudo hierarchy within corporate accounts

    Inefficient Process

    The workaround is not only tedious and time consuming, but there's also a high chance of manual error

    Visibility into Own Data

    Clients have voiced the need for admins with different level of visibility over their respective account's data

Design Challenge

How might we streamline the setup of hierarchical Corporate Accounts for CSMs, while enabling clients to easily view and manage data across all associated child accounts under a single parent structure?

Solution

To streamline onboarding for large enterprise clients, I redesigned 'Parent Account' to support bulk creation of child accounts and enable parent admin access. I also extended parent account visibility to client admins, with a scoped set of permissions tailored to their organizational needs.

New 'Parent Account'

The parent account view was redesigned to support bulk creation of corporate accounts that are automatically linked under the parent. Additionally, we introduced the ability to manage employees' parent admin access, enabling them to view both the parent account and its associated child accounts.

Before vs. After.

Parent and Child Accounts

CSMs can create a parent account by completing the required fields, then generate new child accounts in bulk. These child accounts inherit key information from the parent and are automatically linked under it.

CSMs creating a parent account with linked child accounts.

Parent Admins

Under the new 'Parent Admins' tab, CSMs can assign, modify, or deactivate a client user's admin access to the parent account and all associated child accounts.

CSMs managing client's admin access to their parent account.

'Parent Account' for Client Admins

Once a client user is granted parent admin access, they can view their parent account, a capability previously limited to internal Grubhub admins. While they won’t be able to modify the parent account or its child accounts, they will have visibility into the entire structure and can manage other parent admin users.

External view of 'Parent Account' for client admins.
