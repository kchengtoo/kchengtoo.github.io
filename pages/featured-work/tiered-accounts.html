Tiered Accounts.

Tiered accounts is a parent-to-child relationship that creates a hierarchical structure in Grubhub Corporate Accounts. This allows our onboarding team to create a single parent account, which is used as a template, and create child accounts in bulk under it for our large clients with a tiered structure.

    User & Business Needs

    User: Streamline the process to create a parent account with multiple children
    Business: Obtain the ability to support large Enterprise clients

    Impact

    Unlocked an annual estimate of $1.2 million in Gross Food Sales and reduced the time it takes to create a parent account with 50 child accounts by 95%

    Contribution

    Lead designer in collaboration with PM and engineers

Problem

Within the Corporate Enterprise client base, we have clients with multiple brands, subsidiaries and/or locations within their organization that use Grubhub Corporate Accounts to offer meal benefits for their business. However, due to the lack of hierarchical structure in corporate accounts to support a client's complex organizational structure, our Customer Success Managers (CSMs) have not been able to properly support our large clients. So, we're enhancing the account structure within corporate accounts that'll allow us to support clients with multi-tiered structure.

Challenge: How might we streamline the process to create a Corporate Account that allows CSMs support clients with multi-tiered structure?

Research

I started off by talking to client admins, who are in charge of their company's usage of Grubhub Corporate Accounts, to learn about their needs as large enterprises. Then, I spoke to our CSMs to understand their experience with onboarding large clients onto corporate accounts and, at the same time, evaluate the current state of creating a hierarchical structure within corporate accounts.

    Stakeholder Interview

    Talked to 9 CSMs and 5 client admins of their company's corporate account

    Heuristic Analysis

    Evaluated the current state of tiered accounts creation

Synthesize

After I concluded my research, I identified the main design takeaways that highlight the opportunities to improve the experience of corporate accounts for our large enterprise clients. Here are the design requirements and the HMW statements:

Design Takeaways

    No-True Hierarchy

    CSMs have been relying on a workaround that forms a pseudo hierarchy within corporate accounts

    Inefficient Process

    The workaround is not only tedious and time consuming, but there's also a high chance of manual error

    Visibility into Own Data

    Clients have voiced the need for admins with different level of visibility over their respective account's data

Design Challenge

How might we streamline the process for CSMs to create a Corporate Account with a hierarchical structure and provide clients with visibility into their data across all the child accounts under the parent's umbrella?

Solution

Using Grubhub's design system, I re-designed corporate account's existing "Parent Account" page and added a new flow that'll allow CSMs to create child accounts in bulk under the parent account. Furthermore, external client admins can now view the parent account, which was previously only available for internal admins, but with limited capabilities.

"New" Parent Account

A parent-to-child relationship that allows CSMs to link multiple corporate accounts to a single parent as its children.

A parent account and its child accounts.

Add Child Accounts

Within a parent account, CSMs can create new child accounts in bulk, which inherit details from their parent, and automatically linked to the parent account.

Create child accounts in bulk.

Parent Admins

A new tab called "Parent Admins" where CSMs, and even client admins, can assign admin access to all the child accounts under a parent account.

A list of users with admin access and can add new ones.

Parent Account for Client Admins

Once a client becomes a parent admin, they'll be able to externally view parent accounts with limited functionality, but still has access to its child accounts and parent admins.
