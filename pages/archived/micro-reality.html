Micro Reality

Micro Reality is a stereoscope that allows users to view any interactive stereogram, mimicing the effects of a virtual reality headset. Designed for people who are interested in virtual reality technology. The software is powered by simple web languages, so people with different background in web development can customize it and explore the potential of virtual reality.

2018 Interactive Design
Illustrator, Photoshop, HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Virtual reality headsets can be very expensive.

Virtual reality is an exciting technology, allowing us to immerse ourselves in different environments and experience different realities. However, buying a consumer virtual reality (VR) headset is expensive, costing up to an average of $650. This is not a price you would invest in if you just want to try out this technology. Hence, we created a medium that mimics a VR headset, so everyone gets to experience the virtual reality technology.

Experience virtual environment through a microscope with Micro Reality.

To design Micro Reality, we pinpointed characteristics, strengths and weakness of a VR headset, and then figure out how we can translate them on our "headset's" design. We want our design to be as simple as possible, because Micro Reality is meant to be a medium for trying out virtual reality, so we want the experience to be as straightforward as possible.

Virtual reality technology is based on stereoscopic technique, so we looked at number of sterescope designs and created a couple of concepts. While most stereoscropes are portable, we decided to not make Micro Reality portable, because the motion nature of VR headset often causes nausea. We don't want our users to get sick when using Micro Reality, so we designed our sterescope to be stationary.

Since Micro Reality is an interactive stereoscope, it needs a screen to display the graphics and a viewer to hold the screen. Our stereoscopic viewer design was based on Google Cardboard, but we designed it in a shape similar to a microscope. We want our viewer to fit in any hand-held devices at the end of the viewer. In the case of Micro Reality's design, we decided to use an iPad Mini as the screen, because it has the perfect size for a stationary stereoscope.

Reality can be whatever you want it to be and Micro Reality can make that happen.

Micro Reality is designed to be highly customizable, so everyone can create their own virtual realities. HTML, CSS and JavaScript are ideal programming languages, because they are easy to learn even if the user has no background in programming. We created a web application called "Stereoscopic Planets" to demonstrate the power of Micro Reality.

Exploring the universe may seen impossible, but with Micro Reality, the universe is within your grasp.

Stereoscopic Planets is a web application that displays our solar system. Users can interact with the planets and learn fun facts about each of them. We utilized multiple 3D effects, such as parallax effect, to maximize the immersive experience when viewing it through Micro Reality. Users can customize Stereoscopic Planets through programming, so they could even expand the already-made solar system into an endless universe.

One of the great things about HTML is that you can share it with everyone through the internet.

The potential of Micro Reality is as limitless as the universe.

Micro Reality is a prototype of an interactive stereoscope that is designed to let people try out virtual reality technology without having to buy a VR headset. While Micro Reality tries to mimic the effects of virtual reality to some degree, there are many areas of the design that can be improved to make Micro Reality's experience more immersive. In addition to the expansion plans for Stereoscopic Planets, we also want all the web applications designed for Micro Reality to be open-sourced, so everyone can share their works and experience different realities. This points towards the direction of creating a web platform in which people can upload and download virtual reality applications.
