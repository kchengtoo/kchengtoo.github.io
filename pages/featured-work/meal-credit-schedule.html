Meal Credit Schedule.

Meal Credit Schedule is a setting within the meal benefit program that enables admins to configure when diners can receive their orders paid for with the meal credit. We're improving the flexibility of the delivery window to empower admins to customize, set and forget their meal programs with ease.

    User & Business Needs

    User: Customize, set and forget their meal benefit programs with ease
    Business: Become industry's most flexible meal perks platform

    Impact

    Retained and protected $17+ million in annual spend, and unlocked an annual estimate of $7 million in Gross Food Sales from new client accounts and spend

    Contribution

    Lead designer in collaboration with PM, UX writer and engineers

Problem

Grubhub Corporate's current market claim to fame is that we are "the industry's most flexible meal perks platform". As such, clients expect our meal credit schedule setting to be robust and adaptable to their needs. However, in reality, the feature had limited options to set refresh cadences and delivery windows, which left clients frustrated. So, we're enhancing the schedule setting to expand the options on when corporate diners can receive their orders using the meal credit.

Challenge: How might we enhance the meal credit scheduling feature to increase the flexibility of a meal credit's delivery window permissions?

Process

We broke down the product into multiple milestones based on use case priority, and we've launched the first milestone for the newly designed experience of Day, Week and Custom (Days and Weeks) credit scheduling. This is still an ongoing initiative to expand on other capabilities.

Research

I started off by working with our client success team to talk to client admins and get their thoughts on how the meal credit program is meeting their needs. Then, I evaluated the current state of meal credit creation process to see how client admins are limited by the inflexibility of the existing schedule options.

    Voice of Customers

    Talked and identified 68 clients that gave direct feedback on the meal benefit program

    Heuristic Analysis

    Evaluated the current state of meal credit schedule settings

Synthesize

After conducting my research, I identified key design takeaways that highlight opportunities to improve the experience of meal credit schedule settings. Here are the design takeaways and HMW statement:

Design Takeaways

    Missing Cadences

    Some cadence options are missing which limits admins' desired use cases, e.g. a 3-days long credit

    Inconsistent Options

    Not all cadence options have the same delivery window options, e.g. only daily credits can select a delivery time frame

    Setting Comprehension

    As we introduce more complex use cases, admins need to be able to understand and confirm their settings

Design Challenge

How might we introduce new cadence options and improve the consistency of existing schedule options that enhance the flexibility of the meal credit program without eroding admin's comprehension of it?

Solution

I re-designed the meal credit schedule setting by re-organizing the field placements in consideration of the new cadence and delivery window options, improving field labels to better align with Grubhub's copy standard, and revamping the caption summary for admins to validate if they have customized the schedule settings as intended. Here are some examples of use cases that admins can customize:

Day Setting

Admins can set a meal credit that lasts for a day or refreshes daily. Both options allow admins to specify the delivery time frame, but only a daily credit can specify which days of the week diners can receive their orders paid for with this credit.

A meal credit for a single day.

Week Setting

Admins can set a meal credit that lasts for a week or refreshes weekly. Both options allow admins to specify the time frame and which days of the week diners can receive their orders paid for with this credit.

A meal credit that refreshes weekly.

Custom Days Setting

Admins can set a meal credit that lasts for or refreshes every number of days. This option allows admins to create a credit that lasts more than a day but less than a week, while also specifying the time frame and which days of the week diners can receive their orders paid for with this credit.

A meal credit that refreshes every 3 days.

Custom Weeks Setting

Admins can set a meal credit that lasts for or refreshes every number of weeks. This is currently the largest cadence that a credit can occur and, in both options, admins can also specify the time frame and which days of the week diners can receive their orders paid for with this credit.

A meal credit that refreshes every 3 weeks.
