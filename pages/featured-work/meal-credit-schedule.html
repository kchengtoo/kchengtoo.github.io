Meal Credit Schedule

Meal Credit Schedule is a setting within the meal credit program that enables admins to configure when diners can receive their orders paid for with the meal credit. We're improving the flexibility of the receiving window to empower admins to customize, set and forget their meal programs with ease.

    User & Business Needs

    User: Customize meal credits to accomodate different meal perks use case
    Business: Become the most flexible meal perks program to retain and acquire clients

    Impact

    Retained and protected $17+ million in annual spend, and unlocked an annual estimate of $7 million in Gross Food Sales from new client accounts and spend

    Contribution

    Lead designer in collaboration with PM, UX writer and engineers

Problem

Grubhub Corporate positions itself as "the industry's most flexible meal perks platform," which sets high expectations for adaptability. Clients expect the meal credit scheduling settings to be robust and customizable to match their specific needs. However, the previous experience offered limited options for refresh cadences and receiving windows, leading to frustration among users. To better meet client expectations, we’re enhancing the scheduling settings to provide more flexible and granular control over when corporate diners can place orders using their meal credits.

Challenge: Client admins are frustrated by the lack of flexibility in the meal credit program, as it lacks the necessary configuration options to make it robust and adaptable to their company’s unique meal perk needs.

Process

I collaborated with PM and engineering to break down the initiative into prioritized milestones based on use cases. We prioritized on enhancing the credit scheduling experience for Day, Week, and Custom (Days and Weeks), and introducing new support for Month-based scheduling.

Research

I began by speaking with admins from at-risk clients to understand how well the meal credit program was meeting their needs. From there, I evaluated the current meal credit creation process and identified how the limited scheduling options were falling short for admins.

    Voice of Customers

    Talked and identified 68 clients that gave direct feedback on the meal credit program

    Heuristic Analysis

    Evaluated the current state of meal credit schedule settings

Synthesize

After conducting my research, I identified key design takeaways that highlight opportunities to improve the experience of meal credit schedule settings. Here are the design takeaways and HMW statement:

Design Takeaways

    Missing Cadences

    Some cadence options are missing which limits admins' desired use cases, e.g. a 3-days or month long credit

    Inconsistent Options

    Not all cadence options have the same receiving window options, e.g. only daily credits can select a receiving time frame

    Setting Comprehension

    As we introduce more complex use cases, admins need to be able to understand and confirm their settings

Design Challenge

How might we introduce new cadence options and improve the consistency of existing scheduling features to enhance the flexibility of the meal credit program, while maintaining ease of use for admins?

Solution

I redesigned the meal credit scheduling settings by reorganizing field placements to accommodate new cadence and receiving window options, aligning field labels with Grubhub’s copy standards, and revamping the summary caption to help admins validate their customized schedule settings.

New Scheduling Setting UI

The meal credit scheduling experience was redesigned to be more modern, efficient, and scalable for future enhancements. Labels and fields were organized hierarchically, from general to specific, with clear, descriptive copy to guide users through each step.

Scheduling a Meal Credit

Admins begin by selecting the credit type and cadence. Next, they set the duration by choosing the start and deactivation dates (for recurring credits). Finally, they configure the availability by specifying the days of the month, week, and the time range when orders can be received.

Admins defining the meal credit schedule by completing the relevant fields.

Meal Credit Schedule on the Diner's Side

Once a scheduled meal credit is created, assigned diners will see it in their wallet and at checkout in the Grubhub app, along with details about the receiving window.

Diners viewing their meal credit schedule in Wallet and Checkout.
