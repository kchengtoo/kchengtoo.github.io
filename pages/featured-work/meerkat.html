Meerkat.

Meerkat is a project management platform for innovation teams to streamline and enhance their product development processes by aggregating project data into a single searchable platform.

Time Frame

Problem

Professionals working on innovation projects struggle to manage them across a myriad of digital platforms. Imagine you’re working in an innovation team and have conducted hundreds of user studies across different productivity platforms, like Google Drive, Mural and Jira, but only to find those research notes hidden and unorganized, because you’ve been managing several, siloed digital platforms to complete an innovation project.

Challenge: How might we help innovation teams better manage their research data across a myriad of digital platforms?

Research

I started with a competitive analysis on available solutions for storing user research data, and found that none address insight management for innovation process and many of them have a high barrier to entry. Then, I interviewed professionals in innovation-focused roles to learn about their experience working on innovation projects.

    Competitive Analysis

    Analyzed 5 most commonly used productivity tools

    User Interview

    Conducted 30 minutes sessions with 7 innovation-focused professionals

Synthesize

Once I concluded the interviews, I analyzed my findings to highlight the pain points of insight management during the product development processes and identify the challenge for design. Here are the insights and HMW statement:

Insights

    Lost Insights

    Insights get lost in the innovation process when seperate teams work on different phases at different times

    Poor Information Organization

    Organizing information is a nightmare unless a significant amount of effort is put into it

    Uncertainty with Synthesis

    Users find it difficult to discover actionable insights and recurring themes during a project, especially if it's out of context

Design Challenge

How might we organize information across multiple platforms that allows innovation teams to keep track of insights and make them more discoverable throughout the product development process?

Ideate & Test

Before I went into design, I listed out the design requirements that specified what I wanted my users to experience when using my design, which I used for brainstorming potential solutions.

Design Requirements

    Low Barrier to Entry

    Easy to use for new users, so they won't spend too much time learning how to use the data sharing platform

    Data Aggregation

    Allow relevant work to be easily found from projects across the whole organization in one place

    Easy Data Referencing

    Allow easy search for project research so users can find specific data that they need for their project

High-level Concepts

I developed 3 high-level concepts with the design requirements in mind to see which direction was the most optimal at addressing the design challenge. Here were the 3 concepts:

    Extension / Plug-in

    Desktop Platform

    Mobile App

Concept Testing

I tested the concepts with 6 professionals within innovations teams to hear their thoughts on the 3 concepts:

  • Users were interested in extension solution, but are concerned with the installation and accessibility to research data
  • Users liked the desktop and mobile solutions, but favored the desktop one because they'll most likely be using a computer when working with research

Testing extension solution with participant.

Design & Test II

After testing the concepts with participants, I decided to go forward with the desktop interface as the final solution platform. Then, I created user flows and wireframes to illustrate my design.

User Flow

I created a user flow to outline the goal of the platform. Let's say the professional is a first-time user, the overall goal for them is to:

  • Connect their productivity tools to the platform as they're onboarding
  • Keep track of different projects from a high-level perspective
  • Find projects with the specific insights that they're looking for

Mid-fi wireframes to show the user flow of Meerkat.

Onboarding + File Integration

Professionals can go through the onboarding process to walk them through key features of the Meerkat. While they're onboarding, they can also set up their workspace by connecting their productivity tools to store whiteboards, documents, and other files into Meerkat.

Highlighting key features and connecting productivity tools.

Project Management

Each project has its own dashboard where professionals can see its current phase, activities and team members involved in the project. The dashboard also centralizes the files of the project, like Mural boards, Figma screens and Google documents, so professionals can access them from the platform.

Project dashbooard and opening connected files related to the project.

Global Search

With the documents connected to Meerkat from the integration, professionals can search for people, keywords, projects and content based on project tagging. Professionals can also search with multiple tags to narrow down the results.

Searching for projects with multiple tags.

Usability Testing

I tested the design with 6 professionals within innovations teams and made 3 major improvements based on their feedback:

  • Moved the global search bar to the navigation bar
  • Added the ability to open integrated files as their original format embedded in Meerkat
  • Added high-level information about the project activities

Conclusion

Meerkat was well received by the product managers and they thought it was a great project to continue working on. I learned that when you have a clear user focus, it helps with the research and addressing the problem. One of the biggest challenges was exploring potential directions for an enterprise solution, because it's a professional tool and I had clear design requirements that limited the creativity for idea explorations. So, hopefully in the future I can explore more solutions that are more disruptive.
